What Aria can do

A full assistant, built around your phone.

Calls, messages, verified research, alarms, translation, and more — while voice and AI inference stay on your device.

🎙️

Wake Word

Say "Hey Aria" from anywhere. The always-listening wake word detector runs entirely on-device using OpenWakeWord.

🧠

Gemma 4 On-Device LLM

Powered by Google's Gemma 4 model via LiteRT. Responses are generated locally — no API key, no subscription for inference.

🔊

Natural Voice (Piper TTS)

High-quality text-to-speech via Piper running locally. Choose from multiple voices, adjust speed and pitch.

📱

Phone & Messaging

Make calls, send SMS, read your notifications — all by voice. Premium features, protected by runtime permissions you control.

🌐

Verified Web Research

Aria can search public sources for current facts, compare evidence, cite links, and show an expandable research trace.

🗓️

Calendar & Reminders

Create events, set alarms, and manage reminders with a single voice command. Syncs with your on-device calendar.

📍

Location & Navigation

Ask for directions, local weather, or nearby places. Location is used in-context and never stored or transmitted.

⚙️

Settings Control

Dim the screen, toggle Wi-Fi, adjust volume — full system control via natural voice commands.

🌍

Offline Interface Translation

Choose a language during onboarding or in Settings. ML Kit downloads the model once, then menus translate on-device without a cloud translation request.

🐛

GitHub Issue Reporter

Report a problem from Settings, optionally attach diagnostics or a screenshot, track the issue, and reply to its thread.

How it works

Simple to use. Sophisticated inside.

From wake word to response in under a second — all on your phone.

01

Say "Hey Aria"

The wake word listener runs silently in the background, consuming minimal battery.

02

Aria listens

Whisper-based STT transcribes your voice to text entirely on-device.

03

Gemma 4 processes

The on-device LLM understands your intent and generates a response.

04

Aria speaks back

Piper TTS converts the response to natural speech — no cloud round-trip.

FAQ

Common questions

Everything you need to know about Aria.

Does Aria send my voice or conversations to the cloud?
No. All speech recognition, language model inference, and text-to-speech happen entirely on your device. Your audio is never transmitted to any server. See our Privacy Policy for full details.
Which Android devices are supported?
Aria requires Android 8.0 (API 26) or higher. For the best experience — including GPU-accelerated Gemma 4 inference — a device with at least 6 GB RAM is recommended. Pixel 6 and newer phones work especially well.
How much storage does Aria use?
The app itself is around 80 MB. The default Gemma 4 model is approximately 2.5 GB and is downloaded on first launch. The enhanced Gemma E4B model (Premium) is around 4 GB. You can delete unused models from Settings at any time.
Does Aria work without an internet connection?
Yes — voice, AI inference, and translated menus work offline after their models are downloaded. Internet is still used for current web research, model downloads, billing, Firebase technical telemetry, and issue reports you submit.
What diagnostics does Aria collect?
Firebase Analytics, Crashlytics, and Performance Monitoring collect app interaction and technical reliability/performance data. They do not receive your voice audio or conversation content. See the Privacy Policy for details.
Why does Aria need accessibility permissions?
The optional Accessibility Service lets Aria interact with other apps on your behalf — for example, "tap the Send button in WhatsApp" or "scroll down in Maps." You grant this permission explicitly in Android Settings; Aria never activates it automatically. This permission is not required for core functionality.
Why does Aria need notification access?
Notification access lets you ask things like "What notification did I just get?" or "Read my last WhatsApp message" hands-free. This is an optional permission — the assistant works without it. Access is only used when you actively ask Aria about your notifications.
Can I cancel my Premium subscription?
Yes. Cancel anytime through the Google Play Store under Subscriptions. You retain access until the end of your billing period. We do not offer refunds for partial billing periods, in line with Google Play's policies.
Is Aria open source?
Yes. The full source code is available on GitHub under the Apache 2.0 license. Contributions are welcome.
How does battery usage work?
The wake word listener uses OpenWakeWord on the CPU and consumes approximately 2–5% battery per hour depending on your device. GPU inference for responses is only active while Aria is generating a reply. You can disable the wake word in Settings and trigger Aria manually to minimise battery use.
What data does Aria collect for billing?
Premium subscriptions are processed entirely through Google Play Billing. Aria never sees your payment information. The only data we receive is your anonymised Play purchase token confirming subscription status.
